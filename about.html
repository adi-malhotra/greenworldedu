Key Motivation

More than 200 million children in India are in schools but not learning. This calls for urgent action and whole society mobilization. The cost of inaction is too high: closing doors for a whole generation to lead a meaning life and creation of unequal society with all its inherent problems.

Our Work

GWE aims at improving access to good quality primary education especially for poor underprivileged communities by exploring and implementing innovative, scalable and sustainable solutions/models. This includes experimenting with different models of afterschool learning centers, in-school remedial classes during school hours, different teaching methods, use of different learning materials, and modalities of monitoring studentsâ€™ performance. The organization made the beginning in the under-privileged communities in Northwest Delhi and currently working with about 250 students, most of whom are first generation learners. One of the model that is being tested is establishing standalone supplementary learning centers (called GreenApple learning centers) using standardized teaching learning materials. The other approach is running remedial centers for students lagging behind within the premises of government school itself during regular school hours. The organization is also developing detailed teachers guides for the teachers employed by the organization, but also making them available for government school teachers to improve the overall quality of education in the government schools. It is also exploring how IT based solutions may further improve the access and quality of education.

Finally, it aims to mobilize the community and volunteers and try out innovative ways of engaging people interested in volunteering to reach out to the maximum number of children in need. Over time, we also aims to undertake cutting-edge community-based research to understand impact of different upstream educational policies and programs at the community-level and the social and other positive factors that may enhance learning among students. The work of the organization will be facilitated by an efficient IT-based management information system that will be able to track progress of all the children reached by the organization.
